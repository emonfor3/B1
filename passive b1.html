Understanding the Passive Voice

Passive voice is a grammatical construction where the object of the verb becomes the subject. It is commonly used to emphasize the action rather than the subject or when the subject is unknown or unimportant. In this tutorial, we will cover the passive voice for all tenses and provide examples and practice exercises.

Passive Voice Examples (All Tenses)

Tense Active Voice Passive Voice
Present Simple They make the cake. The cake is made.
Present Continuous They are making the cake. The cake is being made.
Present Perfect They have made the cake. The cake has been made.
Past Simple They made the cake. The cake was made.
Past Continuous They were making the cake. The cake was being made.
Past Perfect They had made the cake. The cake had been made.
Future Simple They will make the cake. The cake will be made.
Future Continuous They will be making the cake. The cake will be being made.
Future Perfect They will have made the cake. The cake will have been made.
